A high-stakes mutiny is brewing inside the halls of Google as a top director dramatically resigned, penning a blistering farewell that accused the tech behemoth of selling its soul to the Pentagon. René Mayrhofer, a director for Android platform security, didn’t just leave—he dropped a morality bomb on his way out, declaring that ‘Google management has lost its moral compass.’

The incendiary internal letter, obtained by SalaciousNews, paints a picture of a company in moral freefall. Mayrhofer claims Google has ‘quietly abandoned’ its carbon-neutral goals to feed its energy-guzzling AI ambitions and, even more damning, is now in bed with the ‘US Ministry of War.’ His scathing critique suggests the company’s infamous ‘Don’t be evil’ motto has been replaced by ‘Don’t ask questions.’

This exodus is a direct response to Google’s clandestine deal to let the Pentagon use its AI models for classified military work—a move that has sparked internal outrage and fierce debate. Mayrhofer, a self-described pacifist, declared he ‘can’t/won’t personally align’ with a direction that could see Google’s technology used for ‘proactively harming people.’ His resignation note reveals a chilling fear: that the same AI tools he helped secure could be turned to mass surveillance against EU citizens, including himself.

The drama exposes a deep and festering wound within Google’s culture. Mayrhofer laments a stark lack of transparency, where earth-shaking decisions are ‘just being decided by top-level management’ without debate. He’s not alone; other conscience-stricken employees have voiced shame over the military pivot, signaling a potential brain drain of the company’s ethically-minded talent.

While Google PR chirps about being ‘proud’ to support national security, this very public defection begs a darker question: Has the search giant, in its quest for supremacy, become a silent partner in the machinery of war? As one exec walks out the door, the industry is left wondering who—or what—will be next.
