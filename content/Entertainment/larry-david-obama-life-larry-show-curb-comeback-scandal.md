He said he was done. He swore it was over. But like a bad habit or a recurring rash, Larry David is back, and his director Jeff Schaffer just spilled the scandalous tea on why: the ‘Curb Your Enthusiasm’ legend has the work ethic of a streetwalker. In a shockingly candid quote that will have HBO’s publicists reaching for the smelling salts, Schaffer compared David to ‘a prostitute who has sex when he’s not working.’ The implication? Larry’s cantankerous genius can’t be switched off; it leaks out, annoying passersby and creating comedy gold whether the cameras roll or not.

And who enabled this glorious return to form? None other than Barack and Michelle Obama! That’s right, the former First Couple’ Higher Ground production company is the unlikely sugar daddy for David’s latest historical tantrum, ‘Life, Larry and the Pursuit of Unhappiness.’ Sources close to the production suggest the Obamas were desperate for a piece of Larry’s irascible magic for America’s 250th birthday—a celebration that, in David’s hands, involves smearing cake on the nation’s face and questioning the very foundations of dessert-sharing.

The show itself is a bombshell of bad taste, rewriting history so that Larry’s pettiness is the secret engine of every major event. Did he inspire Lincoln to go to the theater? Check. Did he draft a miserly, umbrella-hating version of the Declaration of Independence? Obviously. The most scandalous sketch of all features President Obama himself, sharing the screen with David as the comic reportedly gets ‘into a defensive crouch’ over presidential notes. The power dynamic is utterly inverted; the Leader of the Free World is just another schmuck trying to get a word in edgewise with TV’s reigning king of contempt.

Schaffer’s revelations paint a picture of a man constitutionally incapable of peace. The ‘Seinfeld’ co-creator’s bathroom, we are told, is adorned with the Gettysburg Address—not for inspiration, but for efficient time management. Even his urination must be productive! This is the mind that now runs roughshod over the American saga, reducing Rosa Parks’ stand to a seating logistics argument. It’s blasphemous, it’s brilliant, and it’s 100% proof that retirement is for mortals, not for comedy deities who, like the oldest profession, just can’t quit the game.
